20 Year Boy


A sample meal plan for a 20-year-old boy focused on Indian cuisine, allowing non-veg options and promoting overall health:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Fe) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (50 gms) and spinach (25 gms): Provides protein, healthy fats, and essential vitamins (P, H, Vitamins)
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)
  • Fresh fruit juice (200 ml): Provides vitamins and hydration (Vitamins)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing (F, Vitamins)
  • A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and chia seeds for healthy fats, fiber, and B vitamins (H, F, B vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Curry (150 gms) with gravy: Provides protein and iron (P, Fe)
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): A source of complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Roasted Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Fish Tikka (100 gms) with lemon: Provides protein and healthy fats (P, H)
  • Whole wheat crackers (20 gms) with hummus (50 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and protein (F, H, P)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Mutton Rogan Josh (150 gms) with gravy: Provides protein and iron (P, Fe)
  • Jeera Rice (150 gms cooked): Cumin flavored rice for complex carbohydrates (C)
  • Dal Makhani (100 gms): Provides additional protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep and has anti-inflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Eggs: A good source of B12 for vegetarians who consume them
  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12
  • Fortified nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified yogurt and cheese: Check labels for B12 fortification.
  • Consider a Vitamin B12 supplement: Consult a doctor to determine if a supplement is right for you, especially if you have limited dairy or egg intake.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
