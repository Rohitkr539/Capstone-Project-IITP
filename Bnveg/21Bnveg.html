21 Year Boy


A sample meal plan for a 21-year-old boy focusing on Indian cuisine with non-vegetarian options and addressing Vitamin B12 needs:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Fe) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Chicken Omelette (150 gms cooked) with chopped vegetables (onions, spinach) (P, Vitamins, Fe): Provides protein and essential vitamins for a strong start
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with mashed avocado (20 gms) (C, H): Offers complex carbohydrates and healthy fats.
  • Fresh fruit salad (100 gms) with a sprinkle of chia seeds (10 gms) (F, Vitamins, B12): Adds fiber, vitamins, and a touch of B12.

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing (F, Vitamins): Provides a light and refreshing snack.
  • Boiled egg (1 large) (P, Fe, B12): Good source of protein, iron, and B12.

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Mutton Rogan Josh (150 gms) with gravy: Lamb curry rich in protein and iron (P, Fe).
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked) (C): Complex carbohydrates for sustained energy
  • Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H).
  • Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics).

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Fish Tikka (100 gms) with lemon wedge: Grilled fish rich in protein and omega-3 fatty acids (P, H).
  • Roasted peanuts (20 gms): Provides healthy fats and a bit of protein (H, P).

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Chicken Biryani (150 gms cooked): Fragrant rice dish with chicken for protein and complex carbohydrates (P, C).
  • Dal Makhani (150 gms): Lentils cooked with spices, a staple source of protein and fiber (P, F).
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H).

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep and has anti-inflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants, C).

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Eggs: A good source of B12 for vegetarians who consume them.
  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Fortified nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified yogurt and cheese: Check labels for B12 fortification.
  • Consider a Vitamin B12 supplement: Consult a doctor to determine if a supplement is right for you, especially if you have limited dairy or egg intake.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
