25 Year Men


A sample meal plan for a 25-year-old man focusing on Indian cuisine with non-vegetarian options, promoting overall health, and including Vitamin B12 sources:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (50 gms) like spinach, onions, and tomatoes: Provides protein, healthy fats, and vitamins (P, H, Vitamins)
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)
  • Fresh fruit (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (cucumber, carrot) and a lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)
  • A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and chia seeds for healthy fats, fiber, and B vitamins (H, F, B vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Tikka Masala (150 gms) with gravy: Grilled chicken in a flavorful tomato- based gravy, rich in protein (P)
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): A source of complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Dal Tadka (150 gms): Lentils tempered with ghee for protein, fiber, and healthy fats (P, F, H)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Homemade Trail Mix (50 gms): Made with a mix of nuts (almonds, walnuts), seeds (pumpkin seeds), and dried fruits (raisins, cranberries) for a healthy dose of protein, healthy fats, and fiber (P, H, F)
  • Buttermilk (200 ml): Hydrating and good for gut health (Probiotics)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Mutton Rogan Josh (150 gms) with gravy: Lamb cooked in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and iron (P, Iron)
  • Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): Rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources in this plan:

  • Eggs (B12)
  • Chicken Tikka Masala (Chicken is a good source of B12) (B12)
  • Mutton Rogan Josh (Lamb is a good source of B12) (B12)Fortified Yogurt (Some yogurt brands are fortified with B12)
  • Fortified Yogurt (Some yogurt brands are fortified with B12)

Additional notes:

  • While this plan includes natural B12 sources from animal products, consider consulting a doctor about a B12 supplement, especially if your diet is low in animal products or you have absorption issues.
  • Limit processed meats and fried foods.
  • Include healthy fats from sources like nuts, seeds, and olive oil

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
