26 Year Men


A sample meal plan for a 26-year-old man focused on Indian cuisine, rich in protein, healthy fats, and promoting overall health:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Fe) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (50 gms) like spinach, onions, and tomatoes: Provides protein, vitamins, and iron (P, Vitamins, Fe)
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)
  • Fresh fruit (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins and antioxidants (Vitamins, Antioxidants)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Greek yogurt (150 gms) with a handful of mixed nuts and berries (50 gms): Provides protein, calcium, gut health benefits, healthy fats, and B vitamins (P, Ca, Probiotics, H, B vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Tikka Masala (150 gms) with gravy: Grilled chicken in a flavorful tomato- based gravy, rich in protein (P)
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)
  • Small bowl of Dal Makhani (100 gms): Provides extra protein, fiber, and iron (P, F, Fe)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)
  • Roasted Chickpeas (50 gms): A crunchy and protein-rich snack (P)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Fish Curry (150 gms) with Salmon or Sardines: Rich in Omega-3 fatty acids, protein, and Vitamin B12 (P, B12, H)
  • Methi Paratha (1 medium): Flatbread made with whole wheat flour and fenugreek leaves for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and provides a little sweetness (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources (in addition to meat, poultry, and fish):

  • Eggs: A good source of B12, especially the yolk.
  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
