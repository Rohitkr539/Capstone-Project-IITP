27 Year Men


A balanced and nutritious plan can significantly improve your health. Here's a sample meal plan for a 27-year-old man (modify portion sizes based on activity level):

Remember: This is a sample, consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (spinach, onions) (100 gms): Provides protein, healthy fats, and vitamins (P, H, Vitamins)
  • Whole Wheat Toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of Avocado (20 gms): Offers fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)
  • Fresh Fruit (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins and fiber (Vitamins, F)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)
  • Handful of Mixed Nuts (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and cashews for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Curry (150 gms) with boneless, skinless chicken breast: Provides lean protein (P)
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): Source of complex carbohydrates (C)
  • Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil (Vitamins, Minerals, H)
  • Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Fish Tikka (100 gms) with baked or grilled fish: Lean protein and healthy fats (P, H)
  • Roasted Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Mutton Rogan Josh (150 gms) with lean lamb meat: Provides protein and iron (P, Iron)
  • Whole Wheat Roti (2 medium): Flatbread made with whole wheat flour for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Dal Makhani (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm Milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

While most non-veg foods like eggs, fish, and meat are naturally rich in B12, here are some additional options:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Consider a B12 supplement: Consult a doctor to determine if a B12 supplement is necessary, especially if your diet lacks variety or you have absorption issues.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
