30 Year Men


A sample meal plan for a 30-year-old man focusing on Indian cuisine with non-vegetarian options, promoting overall health, and addressing Vitamin B12:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (Fe) - Iron, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (onions, spinach) (100 gms): Provides protein, healthy fats, and essential vitamins (P, H, Vitamins)
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)
  • A glass of fresh fruit juice (250 ml): Provides vitamins and hydration (Vitamins)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Chicken Salad Sandwich (150 gms) with whole wheat bread (2 slices): Shredded chicken breast with chopped vegetables (celery, carrots) in a light mayonnaise dressing provides protein and fiber (P, F)
  • A handful of mixed nuts (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and cashews for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Tikka Masala (150 gms) with gravy: Marinated chicken in a flavorful tomato-based gravy, rich in protein (P)
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): A source of complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Tandoori Gobi (150 gms): Marinated and roasted cauliflower for vitamins and fiber (F, Vitamins)
  • Dahi (yogurt) Raita (100 gms) with chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties, aids digestion, and contains some calcium (Probiotics, Ca)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Fish Fry (100 gms) with lemon juice: Provides lean protein and healthy fats (P, H)
  • Roasted Vegetables (100 gms): Bell peppers, broccoli for additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Mutton Rogan Josh (150 gms): Lamb cooked in a flavorful gravy, a good source of protein and iron (P, Fe)
  • Zeera Rice (150 gms cooked): Cumin flavored rice for complex carbohydrates (C)
  • Palak Paneer (150 gms): Spinach and cottage cheese curry, provides protein, calcium, and iron (P, Ca, Fe)
  • Ghee (1 tsp) for flavor (optional): Use sparingly for a touch of healthy fat (H)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Eggs: A good source of natural Vitamin B12.
  • Fortified Yogurt: Look for yogurt varieties fortified with B12.
  • Fish: Especially Salmon, Sardines, and Mackerel are rich in B12.
  • Chicken and Meat: Provide some amount of B12.
  • Fortified Cereals: Choose cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional Yeast: Sprinkle on salads or add to curries for a deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins.
  • Vitamin B12 supplements: Consider consulting a doctor about a B12 supplement, especially if your diet lacks these options.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals
