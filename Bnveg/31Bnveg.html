31 Year Men


A sample meal plan for a 31-year-old man focused on Indian cuisine with non-vegetarian options, promoting overall health:

Remember: Adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice. Focus on variety and include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits. Drink plenty of water (around 3 liters) daily.

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Fe) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (50 gms) like spinach, onions, and tomatoes: Provides protein, vitamins, and iron (P, Vitamins, Fe)
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms) for healthy fats: Provides fiber and healthy fats (F, H)
  • Fresh fruit (100 gms) like papaya or banana: Provides vitamins and fiber (Vitamins, F)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms) with mixed sprouts (moong dal, Bengal gram) and chopped vegetables (cucumber, carrots) with a lemon dressing: Provides protein, fiber, and vitamins (P, F, Vitamins)
  • A handful of nuts and seeds (20 gms) like almonds and walnuts: Provides healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Curry (150 gms) with boneless, skinless chicken pieces in a flavorful gravy: Provides protein and essential vitamins (P, Vitamins)
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): A source of complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Dal Tadka (150 gms): Lentils cooked with spices, a good source of protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Stir-fried Greens (100 gms) like spinach or kale with a teaspoon of olive oil: Provides essential vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Greek Yogurt (150 gms) with berries (50 gms): Provides protein, calcium, and gut- healthy probiotics (P, Ca, Probiotics)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Fish Tikka Masala (150 gms) with marinated fish pieces in a creamy gravy: Provides protein, healthy fats, and Vitamin D (P, H, Vitamin D)
  • Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): Rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep and has anti-inflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Vitamin B12 Sources:

  • Non-vegetarian sources: Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, and dairy products are naturally rich in B12.
  • Fortified foods: Some breakfast cereals and plant-based milks are fortified with B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries
  • Supplements: Consider consulting a doctor about a B12 supplement if dietary sources are limited.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
