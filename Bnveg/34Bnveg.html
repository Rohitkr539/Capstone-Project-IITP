34 Year Men


A sample meal plan for a 34-year-old man focused on Indian cuisine with non-vegetarian options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Fe) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (onions, spinach) (100 gms): Provides protein, healthy fats, and vitamins (P, H, Vitamins)
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)
  • Fresh fruit (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins and antioxidants (Vitamins, Antioxidants)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)
  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Curry (150 gms) with boneless, skinless chicken breast: Lean protein source (P)
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): Complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): Complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Dahi (yogurt) (100 gms) with chopped cucumber: Provides calcium, gut health benefits, and cooling properties (Ca, Probiotics)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Chicken Keema (minced chicken) Sandwich (100 gms) on whole wheat bread (2 slices): Lean protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Fresh fruit juice (200 ml): Provides additional vitamins and antioxidants (Vitamins, Antioxidants)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Fish Curry (150 gms) with a variety of fish like salmon or mackerel: Rich in omega-3 fatty acids and protein (P, H)
  • Vegetable Pulao (150 gms cooked): Rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Eggs and Dairy Products: These are naturally rich in B12.
  • Fortified Foods: Look for cereals, milk alternatives, and nutritional yeast with added B12.
  • Vitamin B12 Supplements: Consider consulting a doctor about a B12 supplement, especially if your diet lacks eggs and dairy products.

Additional Notes:

  • Limit processed meats (sausages, hot dogs) and red meat consumption.
  • Include healthy fats from sources like olive oil, avocado, and nuts
  • Choose lean protein sources like chicken breast, fish, and lentils.
  • Emphasize whole grains over refined grains for sustained energy.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
