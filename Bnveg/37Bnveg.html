37 Year Men


A sample meal plan for a 37-year-old man focused on Indian cuisine with non-vegetarian options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health. Remember, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Fe) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (50 gms) like spinach, onions, and tomatoes: Provides protein, vitamins, and antioxidants (P, Vitamins, Antioxidants)
  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (50 gms) like spinach, onions, and tomatoes: Provides protein, vitamins, and antioxidants (P, Vitamins, Antioxidants)
  • Glass of milk (200 ml): Provides calcium and vitamin D (Ca, Vitamin D)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms) with mixed sprouts (moong dal, lentil), chopped vegetables (cucumber, carrot), and a lemon dressing: Rich in protein, fiber, and vitamins (P, F, Vitamins)
  • A handful of nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and flaxseeds for healthy fats, fiber, and B vitamins (H, F, B vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Curry (150 gms) with boneless, skinless chicken breast: Lean protein source (P)
  • Chicken Curry (150 gms) with boneless, skinless chicken breast: Lean protein source (P)
  • Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)
  • Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Greek yogurt (150 gms) with mixed berries (50 gms): Provides protein, calcium, gut-healthy probiotics, and antioxidants (P, Ca, Probiotics, Antioxidants)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Fish Fry (150 gms) - Salmon, tuna, or mackerel for healthy fats and omega-3s (H, Omega-3)
  • Methi Paratha (1 medium): A flatbread made with whole wheat flour and fenugreek leaves for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and provides a little sweetness (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Chicken, fish, eggs, and dairy products: Naturally rich in B12.
  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
