45 Year Elder


A balanced plan rich in nutrients can promote overall health for a 45-year-old man. Here's a sample Indian meal plan focusing on non-vegetarian options and including alternatives for Vitamin B12:

Remember: Adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or dietician for personalized advice. Drink plenty of water (around 3 liters daily).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Fe) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Oats (50 gms cooked) with:
  • Chopped fruits (100 gms) like berries or banana (F, Vitamins)
  • Milk (200 ml) or fortified plant-based milk (like soy milk) (Ca, B12)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (onion, cucumber) and a lemon dressing (F, Vitamins): Provides fiber and essential vitamins.
  • Eggs (2 whole) boiled or scrambled: Rich in protein, healthy fats, and B vitamins (P, H, B12)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Curry (150 gms) with gravy: Lean protein source with spices (P)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Spinach Dal (150 gms): Lentils cooked with spinach, rich in protein, fiber, and iron (P, F, Fe)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Fish Tikka (100 gms grilled) with lemon: Lean protein source with Vitamin D (P, Vitamin D)
  • Roasted Makhana (foxnuts) (50 gms): A healthy snack with protein and fiber (P, F)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Mutton Keema (150 gms minced mutton curry): Protein-rich dish with spices (P)
  • Whole Wheat Roti (2 medium): Provides fiber and complex carbohydrates (F, C)
  • Roasted Vegetables (100 gms) like cauliflower, broccoli: Rich in vitamins and antioxidants (Vitamins, Antioxidants)
  • Raita made with curd (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Cooling and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified yogurt or buttermilk: Check labels for B12 fortification
  • Fish: Salmon, sardines, and mackerel are good sources of B12.
  • Chicken and Eggs: Contain some amount of B12.
  • Vitamin B12 supplements: Consider consulting a doctor about a B12 supplement, especially if your diet lacks these options.

Additional Tips:

  • Limit processed foods, sugary drinks, and unhealthy fats.
  • Include a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables for a diverse nutrient profile.
  • Practice portion control and mindful eating.
  • Consider regular exercise for overall well-being.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
