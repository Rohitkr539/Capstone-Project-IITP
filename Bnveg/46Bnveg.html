46 Year Elder Men


A balanced plan rich in nutrients can promote overall health for a 46-year-old man. Here's a sample Indian meal plan focusing on non-vegetarian options and including Vitamin B12 sources:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

  • Omelette (2 eggs) with chopped vegetables (onions, spinach) (100 gms): Provides protein, healthy fats, and vitamins (P, H, Vitamins)
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)
  • Fresh fruit (100 gms): Papaya, banana, or berries for additional vitamins and antioxidants (Vitamins, Antioxidants)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)
  • A handful of mixed nuts (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and cashews for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chicken Curry (150 gms): Boneless chicken cooked in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein (P)
  • Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): A source of complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Roasted Vegetables (100 gms): Broccoli, cauliflower, or carrots for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)
  • Yogurt Raita (100 gms) with chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Fish Tikka (100 gms): Marinated and grilled fish for protein and healthy fats (P, H)
  • Whole Wheat Crackers (20 gms) with a light spread of hummus (50 gms): Provides fiber, protein, and healthy fats (F, P, H)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Mutton Rogan Josh (150 gms): Lamb cooked in a flavorful curry, a good source of protein and iron (P, Iron)
  • Vegetable Pulao (150 gms cooked): Rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Dal Tadka (150 gms): Lentils tempered with spices for additional protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Eggs: A complete protein source rich in B12.
  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Chicken and Mutton Liver: Excellent source of B12, but consume in moderation due to high Vitamin A content.
  • Fish: Especially Salmon, Sardines, and Mackerel are good sources of B12.
  • Fortified Yogurt: Some yogurt brands are fortified with B12.
  • Vitamin B12 supplements: Consider consulting a doctor about a B12 supplement, especially as absorption may decrease with age.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
