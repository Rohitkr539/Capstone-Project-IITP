19 Year Boy


A well-balanced vegetarian diet rich in whole plant-based foods can promote overall health and well-being for a 19-year-old boy. Here's a sample meal plan focusing on Indian cuisine and providing essential nutrients:

Remember: : This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Upma (150 gms cooked): Made with semolina, mixed vegetables (carrot, peas) for a filling and nutritious start

   (F, C, Vitamins)

Dahi (yogurt) (100 gms) with chopped fruits (50 gms): Provides protein, calcium, and gut health benefits (P, Ca, Probiotics)

A handful of nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and chia seeds for healthy fats and B12 (H, B12)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Chickpea salad (100 gms): Boiled chickpeas mashed with chopped vegetables (onion, tomato) and a light lemon dressing

   (P, F, Vitamins)

Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Brown rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Grated beetroot salad (100 gms) with a teaspoon of olive oil: Rich in antioxidants and vitamins (Vitamins, Antioxidants, H)

Buttermilk (200 ml): Hydrating and good for gut health (Probiotics)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Makhana (foxnuts) kheer (150 gms): A sweet dish made with foxnuts, milk, and nuts for protein and healthy fats (P, H)

Seasonal fruit (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Dinner (7 PM):

Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for a complete meal (C, F, Vitamins)

Paneer Tikka Masala (150 gms): Cottage cheese in a creamy gravy, rich in protein and calcium (P, Ca)

Raita made with curd (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch): Promotes restful sleep and has antiinflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.

Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Some types of fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
