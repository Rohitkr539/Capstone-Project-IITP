20 Year Boy


A well-balanced vegetarian diet rich in whole plant-based foods can promote overall health and well-being for a 20-year-old boy. Here's a sample meal plan focusing on Indian cuisine and providing essential nutrients:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Cheela (150 gms cooked): Made with gram flour (besan) and mixed vegetables (spinach, onions) for a protein and fiber-rich

   breakfast (P, F)

Greek yogurt (150 gms) with berries (50 gms): Provides a good amount of protein, calcium, and gut-healthy probiotics (P, Ca,

   Probiotics)

A handful of mixed seeds (20 gms): Chia seeds, flaxseeds, and pumpkin seeds for healthy fats, fiber, and B vitamins (H, F, B vitamins)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon

   dressing (P, F, Vitamins)

Dates (2-3): A natural source of energy with some fiber and minerals (C, F, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

Chole Masala (150 gms): Chickpeas in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): A source of complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Homemade Trail Mix (50 gms): Made with a mix of nuts (almonds, walnuts), seeds (pumpkin seeds), and dried fruits

   (raisins, cranberries) for a healthy dose of protein, healthy fats, and fiber (P, H, F)

Buttermilk (200 ml): Hydrating and good for gut health (Probiotics)

Dinner (7 PM):

Paneer Kofta Curry (150 gms) with gravy: Cottage cheese dumplings in a flavorful curry, rich in protein and calcium (P, Ca)

Methi Paratha (1 medium): A flatbread made with whole wheat flour and fenugreek leaves for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)

Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties,

   and provides a little sweetness (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.

Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.

Some types of fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
