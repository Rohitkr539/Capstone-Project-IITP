23 Year Boy


A well-balanced vegetarian diet rich in whole plant-based foods can promote overall health and well-being for a 23-year-old boy. Here's a sample meal plan focusing on Indian cuisine and providing essential nutrients:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Frittata (150 gms) with chopped spinach, onions, and bell peppers: A protein and veggie-packed breakfast (P, F, Vitamins)

Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with mashed avocado (50 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)

A handful of mixed berries (50 gms): Rich in antioxidants and vitamins (Vitamins, Antioxidants)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (100 gms cooked): Made with sprouted lentils for added protein and digestibility (P, F)

A dollop of low-fat curd (50 gms) with chopped fruits (20 gms): Provides guthealthy probiotics and additional vitamins

   (Probiotics, Vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

Chana Masala (150 gms): Chickpeas in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): A source of complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Gobi Manchurian (150 gms): Cauliflower florets cooked in a spicy and tangy sauce, a low-calorie alternative to meat Manchurian

   (F, Vitamins)

Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Roasted Makhana (foxnuts) (50 gms) with a sprinkle of chaat masala: A light and crunchy snack with a touch of spice (F)

Homemade buttermilk (200 ml): Provides electrolytes for hydration and aids digestion (Electrolytes)

Dinner (7 PM):

Palak Paneer (150 gms): Cottage cheese in a spinach gravy for protein and calcium (P, Ca)

Methi Thepla (2 medium): Flatbread made with whole wheat flour and fenugreek leaves for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)

Tandoori Roasted Vegetables (100 gms): Marinated and grilled vegetables for vitamins, minerals, and a smoky flavor

   (Vitamins, Minerals)

Ghee (1/2 tsp) for flavor (optional): Use sparingly for a touch of healthy fat (H)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory

   properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.

Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.

Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
