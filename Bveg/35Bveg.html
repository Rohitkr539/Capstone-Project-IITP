35 Year Men


A well-balanced vegetarian diet rich in whole plant-based foods can promote overall health and well-being for a 35-year-old Men. Here's a sample meal plan focusing on Indian cuisine and providing essential nutrients:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Sprouts Pancakes (2 medium) made with mixed sprouts (moong dal, Bengal gram) and besan (gram flour) (100 gms):

   Provides protein and fiber for a sustained energy boost (P, F)

Side of chopped fruits (100 gms) and a handful of nuts and seeds (20 gms): Adds essential vitamins,

   minerals, and healthy fats (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Curd (yogurt) with a sprinkle of flaxseeds (150 gms): Promotes gut health with probiotics and adds omega-3 fatty acids (Probiotics, H)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Roasted chickpeas (chana) chaat (50 gms): A crunchy and protein-rich snack with chopped vegetables (onion, tomato)

   and a light lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)

Fresh seasonal fruit (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins and hydration (Vitamins, C)

Lunch (1 PM):

Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, a good source of protein and fiber (P, F)

Brown Basmati Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Stir-fried Tofu (100 gms) with mixed vegetables (broccoli, carrots): Adds protein, vitamins, and minerals (P, Vitamins, Minerals)

Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional

   vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Makhana (foxnuts) kheer (150 gms): A sweet dish made with foxnuts, milk (200 ml), and a pinch of cardamom for protein,

   calcium, and gut health (P, Ca, Probiotics)

Dinner (7 PM):

Paneer Tikka Masala (150 gms): Cottage cheese marinated in spices and cooked in a creamy gravy,

   rich in protein and calcium (P, Ca)

Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)

Raita made with curd (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Ghee (1 tsp) for flavor (optional): Use sparingly for a touch of healthy fat (H)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory

   properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.

Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.

Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
