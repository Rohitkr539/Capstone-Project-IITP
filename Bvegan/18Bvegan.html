18 Year Boy


A sample meal plan for an 18-year-old vegan boy focused on Indian cuisine, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegan Upma (150 gms cooked): Made with semolina flour (sooji) fortified with calcium, cooked with vegetables (carrot, peas) for

   a filling and nutritious start (F, C, Ca)

Soy milk (200 ml) fortified with Vitamin B12 and calcium: Provides protein, B12, and calcium (P, B12, Ca)

A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and chia seeds for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals

   (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a

   lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)

OR

Seasonal fruit (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Palak Paneer (150 gms) with tofu instead of paneer: Spinach gravy with tofu for protein, iron, and calcium (P, Fe, Ca)

Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening snack (4 PM):

Roasted Makhana (foxnuts) (50 gms) with chopped vegetables (onion, tomato) and a sprinkle of chaat masala: A light and crunchy snack with protein and fiber (P, F)

Dates (2-3): A natural source of energy with some fiber and minerals (C, F, Minerals)

Dinner (7 PM):

Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)

Chana Masala (150 gms): Chickpea curry, a good source of protein and fiber (P, F)

Raita made with fortified plant-based yogurt (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties, aids digestion,

   and calcium (Probiotics, Ca)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm plant-based milk (200 ml) fortified with Vitamin B12 and turmeric (a pinch): Promotes restful sleep and has anti-inflammatory

   properties (B12, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified foods: Look for cereals, plant-based milk, and nutritional yeast fortified with B12.

Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Consider consulting a doctor about a Vitamin B12 supplement: Especially if you're strictly vegan to ensure adequate intake.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
