20 Year Boy


A well-planned vegan diet rich in whole foods can promote overall health for a 20-year-old boy. Here's a sample meal plan focusing on Indian cuisine and vegan alternatives:

Remember: : This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice

Focus on Variety: : Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Upma (150 gms cooked): Made with semolina (suji) fortified with calcium, mixed vegetables (carrot, peas) for a filling

   and nutritious start (F, C, Vitamins, Ca)

Soy milk (200 ml) fortified with calcium and Vitamin B12: Provides protein, calcium, and B12 (P, Ca, B12)

A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and chia seeds for healthy fats and B vitamins (H, B vitamins)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (100 gms cooked): Made with sprouted moong dal and vegetables (spinach, onions) for protein and fiber (P, F)

OR

Fresh fruit (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber

   (P, F)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Coconut Chutney (100 gms): Made with fresh coconut, coriander leaves, and spices for added flavor and healthy fats (H)

Evening snack (4 PM):

Homemade Vegan Trail Mix (50 gms): Made with a mix of roasted nuts (almonds, walnuts), seeds (pumpkin seeds), and dried fruits

   (raisins, cranberries) for a healthy dose of protein, healthy fats, and fiber (P, H, F)

Fortified plant-based yogurt (150 gms): Look for varieties with added calcium and B12 (Ca, B12)

Dinner (7 PM):

Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): Rice dish with mixed vegetables, use vegetable stock for added flavor (C, F, Vitamins)

Chana Masala (150 gms): Chickpeas in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Vegan Raita made with plant-based yogurt (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion

   (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

Herbal tea (1 cup): Calming and promotes relaxation

Consider a Vitamin B12 supplement: Consult a doctor to determine the appropriate dosage for your needs (B12)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified foods: Look for cereals, plant-based milk, and nutritional yeast fortified with B12.

Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries (B12)

Disclaimer: : This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
