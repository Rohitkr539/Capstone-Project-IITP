21 Year Boy


A sample meal plan for a 21-year-old vegan boy focused on Indian cuisine, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a registered dietitian for personalized advice. Focus on variety and ensure enough calories for your needs.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Upma (150 gms cooked): Made with semolina and mixed vegetables (carrot, peas) for a filling and nutritious start

   (F, C, Vitamins)

Soy milk (200 ml) fortified with Vitamin B12 and calcium: Provides protein, B12, and calcium (P, B12, Ca)

A handful of mixed berries (50 gms): Provides antioxidants and vitamins (Vitamins, Antioxidants)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Sprouted Moong Dal Salad (100 gms): With chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing for protein and fiber

   (P, F, Vitamins)

OR

Roasted peanuts (20 gms): A good source of protein and healthy fats (P, H)

Lunch (1 PM):

Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Coconut Chutney (50 gms): Made with fresh coconut, coriander leaves, and spices for added flavor and healthy fats (H)

Evening snack (4 PM):

Fruit & Nut Trail Mix (50 gms): Made with a mix of chopped fruits (mango, apple) and nuts (almonds, walnuts) for a healthy dose of

   fiber, protein, and healthy fats (F, P, H)

Vegan yogurt with chopped fruits (150 gms): Provides gut-healthy probiotics and additional vitamins (Probiotics, Vitamins)

Dinner (7 PM):

Chana Masala (150 gms): Chickpea curry, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Jowar Roti (2 medium): Flatbread made with Jowar flour (sorghum) for complex carbohydrates and gluten-free option (C)

Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)

Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm herbal tea (200 ml) with a teaspoon of flaxseed powder: Promotes relaxation and provides some healthy fats (H)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified foods: Look for cereals, plant-based milk, and nutritional yeast fortified with B12.

Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Vitamin B12 supplement: Consider consulting a doctor about a daily B12 supplement (B12).

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
