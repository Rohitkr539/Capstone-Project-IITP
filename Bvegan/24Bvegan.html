24 Year Boy


A well-planned vegan diet rich in whole foods can promote overall health for a 24-year-old boy. Here's a sample meal plan focusing on Indian cuisine and vegan alternatives:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: : Include a rainbow of fruits and vegetables for diverse nutrients.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Upma (150 gms cooked): Made with semolina (sooji) fortified with calcium and iron, mixed vegetables (carrots, peas) for a

   filling start (F, C, Ca, Iron)

Soy milk (200 ml) fortified with B12 and calcium: Provides protein and essential vitamins (P, Ca, B12)

Chia seed pudding (100 gms): Made with chia seeds soaked in plant-based milk (like almond milk) with chopped fruits (berries) for

   fiber, healthy fats, and antioxidants (F, H, Vitamins)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Sprouted moong dal salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (cucumber, tomato) and a lemon dressing: Rich in protein, fiber, and

   Vitamin C (P, F, Vitamin C)

OR

Roasted makhana (foxnuts) (20 gms): A source of protein and healthy fats (P, H)

Lunch (1 PM):

Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Stir-fried Tofu Scramble (100 gms): Scrambled tofu with chopped vegetables (onions, spinach) for protein and essential vitamins

   (P, Vitamins)

Green Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening snack (4 PM):

Fruit Chaat (150 gms): A mix of chopped seasonal fruits like mango, pomegranate, and apples for vitamins and fiber (F, Vitamins)

Plant-based yogurt (100 gms) fortified with B12 and calcium: Provides gut health benefits and additional B12 and calcium

   (Probiotics, B12, Ca)

Dinner (7 PM):

Chana Masala (150 gms): Chickpea curry, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)

Raita made with vegan curd alternative (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion

A teaspoon of flaxseed oil for healthy fats (optional): Provides essential omega-3 fatty acids (H)

Before Bed (optional):

Herbal tea (250 ml) with a teaspoon of honey: Calming and soothing for a good night's sleep (optional: C)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified foods: Look for cereals, plant-based milk, and nutritional yeast fortified with B12.

Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Vitamin B12 supplement: Consult a doctor to determine the appropriate dosage for optimal B12 intake.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
