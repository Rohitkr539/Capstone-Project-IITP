26 Year Men


A well-planned vegan diet rich in whole foods can promote overall health for a 26-year-old man. Here's a sample meal plan focusing on Indian cuisine and vegan alternatives:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: : Include a rainbow of fruits and vegetables for diverse nutrients.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Upma (150 gms cooked): Made with semolina (sooji) fortified with protein and calcium, and mixed vegetables

   (carrot, peas) for a filling start (F, C, Ca)

Soy yogurt (150 gms) with chopped fruits (50 gms): Provides protein, calcium, gut health benefits, and B vitamins

   (P, Ca, Probiotics, B vitamins)

A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Chia seeds, flaxseeds, and walnuts for healthy fats, fiber, and B vitamins

   (H, F, B vitamins)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon

   dressing (P, F, Vitamins)

OR

Dates (2-3): A natural source of energy with some fiber and minerals (C, F, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked w

Coconut Chutney (100 gms): Made with fresh grated coconut, coriander leaves, and spices for added flavor and healthy fats (H)

Evening snack (4 PM):

Homemade Vegan Trail Mix (50 gms): Made with a mix of roasted peanuts, pumpkin seeds, dried fruits (raisins, cranberries)

   for protein, healthy fats, and fiber (P, H, F)

Vegan Milk (250 ml) fortified with B12 and Calcium: Provides hydration and essential nutrients (Ca, B12)

Dinner (7 PM):

Chana Masala (150 gms): Chickpea curry, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Missi Roti (2 medium): Flatbread made with whole wheat flour and sprouted black gram (urad dal) for complex carbohydrates

   and protein (C, P)

Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm herbal tea (250 ml) with a teaspoon of flaxseed oil: Promotes relaxation and provides some healthy fats (H)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified Vegan Milk and Cereals: Look for products with added B12.

Nutritional Yeast: A deactivated yeast rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or curries

Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.

Vitamin B12 Supplements: Consider consulting a doctor for a daily B12 supplement to ensure adequate intake.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
