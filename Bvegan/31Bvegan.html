31 Year Men


A well-planned vegan diet rich in whole foods can promote overall health for a 31-year-old man. Here's a sample meal plan focusing on Indian cuisine and vegan alternatives:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: : Include a rainbow of fruits and vegetables for diverse nutrients.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Upma (150 gms cooked): Made with semolina (sooji) fortified with protein and calcium, and mixed vegetables (carrot, peas)

   for a filling start (F, C, Ca)

Dalia Porridge (150 gms cooked) with chopped fruits (50 gms): Cracked wheat porridge with fruits like berries or banana for

   sustained energy and fiber (C, F, Vitamins)

Plant-based milk (200 ml) fortified with Vitamin B12 and Calcium: Provides protein alternative and essential nutrients

   (P, Ca, B12)

A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and chia seeds for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals

   (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Sprouted moong dal salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber, onion) and a lemon dressing

   (P, F, Vitamins)

OR

Vegan smoothie (250 ml) made with plant-based milk, banana, spinach, and a scoop of vegan protein powder

   (P, C, Vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Stir-fried Tofu Scramble (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (onions, bell peppers) for added protein and vitamins

   (P, Vitamins)

Green salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins

   and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening snack (4 PM):

Roasted makhana (foxnuts) (50 gms) with a handful of mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts) (20 gms) for protein and

   healthy fats (P, H)

Fruit Chaat (100 gms) with a variety of seasonal fruits: Provides essential vitamins and fiber (Vitamins, F)

Dinner (7 PM):

Chana Masala (150 gms): Chickpea curry, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)

Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber

   (C, F)

Raita made with vegan yogurt (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion

   (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm herbal tea (250 ml) with a teaspoon of honey (optional): Promotes relaxation (Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified Vegan Milk and Cereals: Look for products with added B12.

Nutritional Yeast: A deactivated yeast rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or curries

Vitamin B12 supplements: Consider consulting a doctor about a B12 supplement, especially for long-term vegans, to

   ensure adequate intake.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
