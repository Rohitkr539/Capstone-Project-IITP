19 Year Girl


A sample meal plan for a 19-year-old girl focused on Indian cuisine, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation with some non-vegetarian options:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Omelette (150 gms cooked) with spinach, onions, and tomatoes: Provides protein and essential vitamins

   (P, Vitamins)

Whole Wheat Toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins

   (F, H, B vitamins)

Seasonal Fruit (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Sprouted Moong Dal Salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (cucumber, carrot) and a lemon dressing: Provides protein,

   fiber, and vitamins (P, F, Vitamins)

A handful of nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and pumpkin seeds for healthy fats, fiber, and B vitamins

   (H, F, B vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

Chicken Curry (150 gms) with gravy: Made with chicken breast and vegetables, a good source of protein and iron (P, Fe)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Greek yogurt (150 gms) with mixed berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of granola (20 gms): Provides protein, calcium, gut health

   benefits, and fiber (P, Ca, Probiotics, F)

Dinner (7 PM):

Salmon Fish (150 gms baked): Rich in omega-3 fatty acids, protein, and Vitamin B12 (P, B12, H)

Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber

   (C, F)

Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties,

   and provides a little sweetness (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Menstruation Relief:

Iron-rich foods: During menstruation, iron loss is common. Include iron-rich foods throughout your cycle like chicken, fish,

   lentils, spinach. (Fe)

Calcium-rich foods: Calcium helps with cramps. Include dairy products like curd, yogurt, or fortified plant-based milk. (Ca)

Warm liquids: Herbal teas like chamomile or ginger tea can help with cramps.

Hydration: Drink plenty of water to prevent bloating and constipation.

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.

Eggs: A good source of Vitamin B12 for non-vegetarians (B12)

Fortified nutritional yeast: Sprinkle on salads or add to curries.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
