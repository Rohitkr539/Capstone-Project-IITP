21 Year Girl


A sample meal plan for a 21-year-old girl focused on Indian cuisine, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation with some relief options:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Omelette (150 gms cooked) with spinach, onions, and tomatoes: Provides protein and essential vitamins

   (P, Vitamins)

Whole Wheat Toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins

   (F, H, B vitamins)

Glass of milk (200 ml): Provides calcium and Vitamin B12 (Ca, B12)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Yogurt Parfait (150 gms): Layered with Greek yogurt (100 gms), chopped fruits (berries, banana) (50 gms), and a sprinkle of

   granola (20 gms) for protein, fiber, healthy fats, and gut health (P, F, H, Probiotics).

Lunch (1 PM):

Chicken Curry (150 gms) made with boneless chicken pieces: Rich in protein and iron (P, Fe)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H).

Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Menstruation Relief options:

Include a cup of herbal tea like chamomile or ginger tea during your period for cramps and relaxation.

Consider a small piece of dark chocolate (20 gms) for a mood boost.

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Sprouts Salad (100 gms): A mix of sprouted moong dal and lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and

   a lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)

A handful of mixed nuts (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and cashews for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals

   (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Dinner (7 PM):

Fish curry (150 gms) made with Salmon or Sardines: Excellent source of protein, healthy fats, and Vitamin B12 (P, H, B12)

Vegetable Pulao (150 gms cooked): Rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)

Steamed vegetables (100 gms) like broccoli or cauliflower: Provides essential vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Before Bed (optional):

Warm milk (200 ml) with a pinch of turmeric: Promotes restful sleep and has anti inflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants)

Menstruation Relief option :

Warm milk with a pinch of turmeric before bed can help with cramps and sleep

Include iron-rich foods like spinach or lentils in your dinner for iron loss during menstruation (Fe).

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.

Eggs: A good source of Vitamin B12 for non-vegetarians (B12)

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
