22 Year Girl


A sample meal plan for a 22-year-old girl focused on Indian cuisine, rich in nutrients, and including non-vegertarian options to promote overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Omelette (150 gms cooked) with mixed vegetables (onions, spinach): Provides protein and essential vitamins

   (P, Vitamins)

Whole Wheat Toast (2 slices) with mashed avocado (50 gms): Healthy fats, fiber, and B vitamins (H, F, B vitamins)

Milk (200 ml) or fortified plant-based milk (like soy milk): Provides calcium and B12 (Ca, B12)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Greek Yogurt (100 gms) with mixed berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of nuts (almonds, walnuts) (20 gms): Provides protein,

   calcium, gut-healthy probiotics, healthy fats, and fiber (P, Ca, Probiotics, H, F)

Lunch (1 PM):

Chicken Curry (150 gms) with gravy: Lean protein source rich in iron (Fe, P)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Additional vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Sprouts Salad (100 gms): Moong dal or lentil sprouts with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing

   (F, Vitamins)

Handful of mixed nuts (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and cashews for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Dinner (7 PM):

Fish Fry (150 gms) with lemon: Lean protein, healthy fats (omega-3s) (P, H)

Methi Paratha (1 medium): Flatbread made with whole wheat flour and fenugreek leaves for complex carbohydrates and fiber

   (C, F)

Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Before Bed (optional)

Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties,

   and provides a little sweetness (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Menstruation cycle Relief foods:

Iron-rich foods: Lean meats, fish, eggs, lentils, chickpeas (Fe)

Calcium-rich foods: Dairy products like yogurt, paneer, green leafy vegetables (Ca)

Fruits rich in Vitamin C and B6: Papaya, oranges, bananas (helps with iron absorption) (Vitamins B6, C)

Warm liquids: Herbal teas like chamomile or ginger tea (soothes cramps)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

Eggs (2 large eggs provide almost 50% of daily B12 needs): A complete protein source with B12 (P, B12).

Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.

Fortified nutritional yeast: Sprinkle on salads or add to curries for B vitamins (B vitamins).

Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
