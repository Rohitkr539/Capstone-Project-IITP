30 Year Women


A sample meal plan for a 30-year-old woman focused on Indian cuisine with non-veg options, rich in nutrients, and addressing menstrual relief:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Scrambled Eggs (2 whole eggs) with chopped spinach (50 gms) and a slice of whole wheat toast (30 gms): Provides protein, iron,

   and B vitamins for energy (P, Fe, B12)

Seasonal fruit (100 gms): Provides vitamins and antioxidants (Vitamins, Antioxidants)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Greek Yogurt Parfait (150 gms) with chopped berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of nuts and seeds (20 gms): Provides protein, calcium,

   gut health benefits, and healthy fats (P, Ca, Probiotics, H)

Lunch (1 PM):

Chicken Curry (150 gms) with boneless, skinless chicken breast: Rich in protein and B vitamins (P, B12)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Roasted Vegetables (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Salmon Tikka (100 gms) with a side of cucumber raita (100 gms): Provides healthy fats, protein, and B vitamins (P, H, B12)

Dinner (7 PM):

Mutton Keema (minced lamb) (100 gms) with whole wheat roti (2 medium): Provides protein and iron (P, Fe)

Dal Makhani (kidney beans and lentils) (150 gms): Provides protein, fiber, and iron (P, F, Fe)

Stir-fried Broccoli (100 gms): Provides vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Before Bed (optional)

Warm Milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory

   properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Menstruation cycle Relief :

Iron-rich foods: Lean red meat, chicken, fish, lentils, spinach (Fe)

Calcium-rich foods: Yogurt, paneer, leafy greens (Ca)

Ginger tea: Soothes cramps (Anti-inflammatory)

Fruits rich in Vitamin C: Oranges, grapefruits, strawberries (Vitamin C) - helps with iron absorption

Warm liquids: Herbal teas, broth - helps with cramps and bloating

Vitamin B12 sources:

Eggs: Rich source of B12 (whole egg) (B12)

cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.

Fortified Foods: Some breakfast cereals and nutritional yeast may be fortified with B12.

Fish: Especially Salmon, Sardines, and Mackerel (B12)

Meat: Chicken, Lamb, and Beef (B12)

Vitamin B12 supplements: Consider consulting a doctor about a B12 supplement to ensure adequate intake.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
