38 Year Women


A sample meal plan for a 38-year-old woman focused on Indian cuisine, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation.

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Omelette (150 gms cooked) with spinach, onions, and tomatoes: Provides protein and essential vitamins (P, Vitamins)

Whole Wheat Toast (2 slices) with mashed avocado (50 gms): A good source of fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)

A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and flaxseeds for B vitamins, healthy fats, and minerals

   (B12, H, Minerals)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Greek yogurt (150 gms) with mixed berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of pumpkin seeds (20 gms): Provides protein, gut health benefits,

   healthy fats, and fiber (P, Probiotics, H, F)

Lunch (1 PM):

Chicken Curry (150 gms) with gravy: Lean protein source rich in iron (Fe, P)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Lentil Salad (100 gms): Provides fiber, protein, and iron (F, P, Fe)

Spinach stir-fried with a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Rich in iron, magnesium, and vitamins (Fe, Mg, Vitamins)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Salmon Tikka (100 gms) with lemon: Rich source of omega-3 fatty acids and protein (H, P)

Roasted chickpeas (50 gms): Provides protein, fiber, and iron (P, F, Fe)

Dinner (7 PM):

Mutton Keema (minced lamb) Curry (150 gms) with gravy: Iron-rich protein source (Fe)

Whole Wheat Roti (2 medium): Provides complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)

Roasted Vegetables (100 gms): Offer essential vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Raita made with curd (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional)

Warm Milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory

   properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants)

Menstruation cycle Relief :

Iron-rich foods: Consume iron-rich foods throughout your cycle, especially during menstruation. Examples include chicken, fish,

   lentils, and green leafy vegetables (Fe).

Magnesium-rich foods: Magnesium helps with cramps and mood swings. Include almonds, bananas, green leafy vegetables.

Hydration: Staying hydrated helps with bloating and cramps.

Ginger: Ginger tea can help ease cramps.

Vitamin B12 sources:

Eggs: Rich source of B12 (whole egg) (B12)

Fortified Foods: Some breakfast cereals and nutritional yeast may be fortified with B12.

Fish: Especially Salmon, Sardines, and Mackerel (B12)

Meat: Chicken, Lamb, and Beef (B12)

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
