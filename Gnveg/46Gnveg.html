46 Year Elder Women


A sample meal plan for a 46-year-old woman focused on Indian cuisine, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menopause.

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters).

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12

Morning (7 AM):

Vegetable Omelet (150 gms cooked) with 2 eggs and mixed vegetables (onions, spinach): Provides protein, healthy fats, and

   vitamins (P, H, Vitamins)

Whole Wheat Toast (2 slices) with a thin layer of Avocado (20 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins

   (F, H, B vitamins)

Warm milk (200 ml) with a teaspoon of turmeric: Promotes bone health and has anti-inflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

Greek Yogurt (150 gms) with mixed berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of nuts (almonds, walnuts) (20 gms): Provides protein,

   gut health benefits, healthy fats, and B vitamins (P, Probiotics, H, B vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

Chicken Curry (150 gms) with boneless, skinless chicken breast: Lean protein source (P)

Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)

Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H )

Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals

   (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

Roasted Makhana (foxnuts) (50 gms) with a handful of mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts) (20 gms): Provides healthy fats,

   protein, and fiber (H, P, F)

Fresh fruit (100 gms) like apple or pear: Provides essential vitamins and fiber (Vitamins, F)

Dinner (7 PM):

Salmon (150 gms baked): Rich in protein and omega-3 fatty acids for heart health (P, H)

Moong Dal Khichdi (150 gms cooked): Split mung lentils and brown rice dish for easy digestion and fiber (F)

Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Raita made with curd (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional)

Chamomile tea (1 cup): Promotes relaxation and sleep (optional)

Menstruation cycle Relief :

Iron-rich foods: During menstruation, iron loss is common. Include iron-rich options like chicken liver (100 gms - 11.7 mg Fe),

   chickpeas (100 gms - 3.6 mg Fe), and green leafy vegetables (spinach) throughout your cycle

Calcium-rich foods: Yogurt, paneer, leafy greens (Ca)

Fruits rich in Vitamin C: Oranges, grapefruits, strawberries (Vitamin C) - helps with iron absorption

Vitamin B12 sources:

Eggs: Rich source of B12 (whole egg) (B12)

Chicken: Liver and dark meat (good sources)

Fish: Especially Salmon, Sardines, and Mackerel (B12)

Fortified Foods: Some breakfast cereals, nutritional yeast

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
