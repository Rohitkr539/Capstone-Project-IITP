21 Year Girl


A sample meal plan for an 21-year-old girl focused on Indian cuisine with vegetarian options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Vegetable Frittata (150 gms) with chopped spinach, tomatoes, and onions: Provides protein and essential vitamins (P, Vitamins)
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with mashed avocado (50 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)
  • Milk (200 ml) or fortified plant-based milk (like soy milk) (Ca, B12)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and a lemon dressing: Provides protein, fiber, and vitamins (P, F, Vitamins)
  • Handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and pumpkin seeds for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)
  • Raita made with curd (100 gms) and mint chutney: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Greek yogurt Parfait (150 gms) with berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of granola (20 gms): Provides protein, fiber, healthy fats, and gut health (P, F, H, Probiotics)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Paneer Tikka Masala (150 gms): Cottage cheese in a creamy gravy, rich in protein and calcium (P, Ca)
  • Methi Roti (1 medium): Flatbread made with whole wheat flour and fenugreek leaves for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep and has anti-inflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

Pre-Menstrual Phase (Week before Period):

  • Focus on iron-rich foods like dark leafy greens (spinach), lentils, chickpeas (Iron)
  • Include mood-boosting foods like bananas, yogurt with berries (rich in tryptophan, a precursor to serotonin)
  • Consider herbal teas like chamomile or peppermint for cramps (consult a doctor before using herbs)

Menstrual Phase (During Period):

  • Stay hydrated with plenty of water and herbal teas like ginger tea (may help with cramps)
  • Choose easily digestible foods like steamed vegetables, brown rice, and khichdi (a lentil and rice dish) (F, C)
  • Include warming spices like ginger and cinnamon in your meals for potential cramp relief

Post-Menstrual Phase (Week after Period):

  • Refill iron stores with dals, leafy greens, and fortified cereals (Iron)
  • Choose energizing foods like whole grains, sweet potatoes, and nuts for sustained energy (C, H)
  • Include healthy fats like avocado and olive oil for hormone regulation (H)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
