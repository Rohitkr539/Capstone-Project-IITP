24 Year Girl


A sample meal plan for an 24-year-old girl focused on Indian cuisine with vegetarian options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Vegetable Frittata (150 gms) with chopped spinach, mushrooms, tomatoes, and onions: Provides protein and essential vitamins (P, Vitamins)
  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices) with mashed avocado (50 gms): Provides fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (F, H, B vitamins)
  • Milk (200 ml) or fortified plant-based milk (like soy milk) (Ca, B12)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Greek yogurt (100 gms) with mixed berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of granola (20 gms): Provides protein, fiber, healthy fats, and gut health (P, F, H, Probiotics)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chickpea Curry (Chole) (150 gms): Rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Salad with mixed greens, chopped vegetables (cucumber, tomato), and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Roasted Makhana (foxnuts) (50 gms) with a handful of mixed nuts (20 gms): Provides protein, healthy fats, and fiber (P, H, F)
  • Fruit (100 gms) like apple or pear: Provides vitamins and fiber (Vitamins, F)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Paneer Tikka Masala (150 gms): Cottage cheese in a creamy gravy, rich in protein and calcium (P, Ca)
  • Methi Paratha (1 medium): Flatbread made with whole wheat flour and fenugreek leaves for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)
  • Ghee (1 tsp) for flavor (optional): Use sparingly for a touch of healthy fat (H)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep and has anti-inflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

During Menstruation:

  • Focus on iron-rich foods to combat potential deficiencies (Fe)
  • Include: Sprouted lentils or moong dal (100 gms cooked) with chopped vegetables (spinach, carrots) for iron and fiber (Fe, F).
    Dark chocolate (20 gms) for magnesium and mood regulation (Magnesium).
    Beetroot (100 gms) roasted or boiled for iron and folate (Fe, Folate).
    Warm herbal tea (like chamomile or ginger) to soothe cramps (Antioxidants).

Pre-Menstruation (PMS):

  • Include foods rich in calcium and magnesium to help with mood swings and bloating.
    Sesame seeds (1 tablespoon) sprinkled on salads or yogurt for calcium (Ca).
    Bananas (1 medium) for potassium and mood regulation (Potassium).
    Leafy greens (spinach, kale) (100 gms) for magnesium and vitamins (Magnesium, Vitamins).
    Yogurt (100 gms) with berries (50 gms) for gut health and probiotics (Probiotics).

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
