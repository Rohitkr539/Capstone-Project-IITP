28 Year Women


A sample meal plan for an 28-year-old Women focused on Indian cuisine with vegetarian options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (1 medium) with chopped vegetables (50 gms): Provides protein, fiber, and vitamins (P, F, Vitamins)
  • Greek Yogurt (150 gms) with berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of chia seeds (1 tbsp): Provides protein, calcium, gut health, and healthy fats (P, Ca, Probiotics, H)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Seasonal Fruit (100 gms) with a handful of nuts (20 gms): Provides vitamins, minerals, and healthy fats (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Vegetable Pulao (150 gms cooked): Rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Dal Makhani (150 gms): Lentils cooked with spices, a good source of protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Raita made with curd (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms):Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Makhana Kheer (150 gms): A sweet dish made with foxnuts, milk, and nuts for protein, healthy fats, and calcium (P, H, Ca)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Paneer Tikka Masala (150 gms): Cottage cheese in a creamy gravy, rich in protein and calcium (P, Ca)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Roasted Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)
  • Ghee (1 tsp) for flavor (optional): Use sparingly for a touch of healthy fat (H)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of honey: Promotes restful sleep and has anti-inflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants, C)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

Pre-Menstrual Phase (Week before Period):

  • Iron-rich foods (daily):Dark leafy greens (spinach, kale) (100 gms), lentils (100 gms cooked), chickpeas (100 gms cooked) - to support iron levels (Fe)
  • Calcium-rich foods (daily): Sesame seeds (1 tbsp), fortified plant-based milk (200ml) - to support mood regulation (Ca)
  • Ginger tea (2 cups daily): May help with cramps

Menstrual Phase (During Period):

  • Warm liquids: Herbal teas (like chamomile or ginger), warm water with lemon - for hydration and cramps
  • Fruits rich in Vitamin C (daily): Papaya (100 gms), orange (1 medium) - to support immunity
  • Comforting foods (in moderation): Vegetable soup (1 cup), homemade dark chocolate (20 gms) - for mood and cravings

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
