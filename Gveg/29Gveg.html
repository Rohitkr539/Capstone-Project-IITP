29 Year Women


A sample meal plan for an 29-year-old Women focused on Indian cuisine with vegetarian options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (2 medium, 100 gms cooked) with chopped vegetables (onions, tomatoes) (P, F, Vitamins): A protein and fiber-rich breakfast.
  • Greek Yogurt (150 gms) with berries (50 gms): Provides protein, calcium, and gut-healthy probiotics (P, Ca, Probiotics).
  • Flaxseeds (1 tbsp, 10 gms): Rich in omega-3 fatty acids and fiber (H, F).

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Seasonal Fruit (1 cup, 150 gms): Provides essential vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals).
  • Handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and pumpkin seeds for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals (H, B vitamins, Minerals).

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Vegetable Pulao (1 cup cooked, 150 gms): Rice dish with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F).
  • Dal Makhani (1 cup, 150 gms): Kidney beans and lentils in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F).
  • Spinach Raita (1 cup, 150 gms) with curd and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties, aids digestion, and contains calcium (Probiotics, Ca).
  • Small side salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H).

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Makhana Kheer (1 cup, 150 gms): A sweet dish made with foxnuts, milk, and nuts for protein and healthy fats (P, H).
  • Herbal Tea (1 cup): Choose calming options like chamomile or lavender for relaxation.

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Paneer Tikka Masala (1 cup, 150 gms): Cottage cheese in a creamy gravy, rich in protein and calcium (P, Ca).
  • Brown Basmati Rice (1 cup cooked, 150 gms): A source of complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C).
  • Roasted Vegetables (150 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)
  • Ghee (1 tsp) for flavor (optional): Use sparingly for a touch of healthy fat (H)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and supports stress management (Ca, Antioxidants).

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

Focus on including these throughout your cycle, especially during PMS:

  • Iron-rich foods (daily): Dark leafy greens (spinach, kale), lentils, chickpeas, fortified cereals (Fe).
  • Calcium-rich foods (daily): Dairy products like yogurt and cheese (if consumed), fortified plant-based milk, leafy greens (Ca).
  • Omega-3 fatty acids (2-3 times a week): Flaxseeds, walnuts, chia seeds (H).
  • Fruits rich in Vitamin C (daily): Oranges, grapefruits, strawberries (Vitamin C).
  • Ginger tea: Soothes cramps and nausea.
  • Warm compress on the abdomen: Provides pain relief.

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
