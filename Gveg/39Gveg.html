39 Year Women


A sample meal plan for an 39-year-old Women focused on Indian cuisine with vegetarian options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Whole wheat toast (2 slices, 50 gms) with mashed avocado (50 gms) and a sprinkle of flaxseeds (1 tbsp, 10 gms) (F, C, H, B vitamins)
  • Scrambled tofu (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (tomato, spinach) (P, Vitamins)
  • Milk (200 ml) or fortified plant-based milk (Ca, B12)
         OR
  • Vegetable poha (150 gms cooked) with chopped peanuts (20 gms) for a lighter option (F, P, C)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Greek yogurt (100 gms) with mixed berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of granola (20 gms): Provides protein, fiber, healthy fats, and gut health (P, F, H, Probiotics)
         OR
  • Sprouted moong dal salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (cucumber, carrot) and a lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Dal Makhani (150 gms) with kidney beans and lentils (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (100 gms cooked) or Jowar roti (1 medium) (c)
  • Palak Paneer (150 gms) with spinach and cottage cheese (P, Ca)
  • Roasted seasonal vegetables (100 gms) (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms) (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Makhana (foxnuts) kheer (100 gms) with a sprinkle of nuts and chopped fruits (P, H, Vitamins)
         OR
  • Dates (2-3) with a handful of mixed nuts (20 gms) (C, F, H, Minerals)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked) with a mix of vegetables for a complete meal (C, F, Vitamins)
  • Chole Masala (150 gms) with chickpeas in a flavorful gravy (P, F)
  • Methi Paratha (1 medium) with whole wheat flour and fenugreek leaves (C, F)
  • Stir-fried greens (100 gms) like spinach or kale for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder (Ca, Antioxidants, stress management)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

Focus on:

  • Anti-inflammatory foods: Ginger (1-inch piece), turmeric (half tsp), green tea (1 cup)
  • Warming foods: Sesame seeds (1 tbsp), jaggery (1 tsp)
  • Foods rich in iron and calcium: Dark leafy greens (spinach, kale), lentils, nuts and seeds, fortified plant-based milk (Fe, Ca)
  • Mood-boosting foods: Dark chocolate (50 gms), fruits (berries, banana)

Sample Period Relief Options:

  • Ginger tea with honey: Soothes cramps and nausea (Anti-inflammatory)
  • Sesame seed laddoos with jaggery: Provides energy and iron (Iron, C)
  • Spinach and lentil soup: Rich in iron and protein (Fe, P)
  • Yogurt with berries and dark chocolate: Promotes gut health and mood (Probiotics, Antioxidants)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
