46 Year Elder Women


A sample meal plan for an 46-year-old Women focused on Indian cuisine with vegetarian options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Soaked Almonds (10 gms) and Chia Seeds (1 tbsp) with milk (200 ml) or fortified plant-based milk (B12, Ca, H)
         OR
  • Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (1 medium) with chopped vegetables (spinach, onions) for protein and fiber (P, F)
  • A piece of fruit (100 gms) like apple or banana (Vitamins, C)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Low-fat yogurt (100 gms) with berries (50 gms) for protein, gut health, and antioxidants (P, Probiotics, Antioxidants)
         OR
  • Roasted makhana (foxnuts) (20 gms) with a sprinkle of mixed herbs for a light and crunchy snack (H)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Dal Makhani (150 gms) with kidney beans and lentils (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (100 gms cooked) or Jowar roti (1 medium) (c)
  • Steamed Vegetables (150 gms) like broccoli, cauliflower for vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms) for additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Vegetable and fruit salad (150 gms) with a light lemon dressing for vitamins, fiber, and hydration (Vitamins, F)
         OR
  • A handful of mixed nuts (20 gms) for healthy fats and minerals (H, Minerals)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Paneer Tikka Masala (150 gms) with minimal oil for protein and calcium (P, Ca)
  • Jowar roti (2 medium) made with Jowar flour (sorghum) for complex carbohydrates and gluten-free option (C)
  • Roasted seasonal vegetables (150 gms) for essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)
  • Small portion of brown rice or quinoa (50 gms cooked) for additional complex carbohydrates (optional) (C)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) for a calming effect and antiinflammatory properties (Ca, Antioxidants)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

  • Flaxseeds (1 tbsp) ground and soaked in water for 30 mins: Rich in plantbased lignans that may help manage hormonal fluctuations (can be added to smoothies or yogurt).
  • Soy products (tofu, tempeh) (100 gms): Provide plant-based isoflavones with similar properties to estrogen.
  • Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower) (150 gms): May help with estrogen metabolism.
  • Fruits rich in Vitamin C (100 gms) like oranges, guava: Important for overall health and hormone regulation.
  • Healthy fats (avocado, olive oil): Promote hormonal balance and satiety.

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Tempeh: A fermented soybean product containing a small amount of B12.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
