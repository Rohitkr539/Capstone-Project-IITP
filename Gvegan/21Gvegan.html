21 Year Girl


A sample meal plan for an 21-year-old girl focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Vegetable Cheela (150 gms cooked): Made with gram flour (besan) and mixed vegetables (spinach, onions) for a protein and fiber-rich breakfast (P, F)
  • Soy yogurt (150 gms) with berries (50 gms): Provides protein, calcium alternative, and gut health benefits (P, Ca, Probiotics)
  • Chia seed pudding (100 gms): Made with chia seeds, plant-based milk, and chopped fruits for fiber, protein, and healthy fats (F, P, H)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Fruit & Nut Trail Mix (50 gms): Made with a mix of dried fruits (cranberries, raisins), nuts (almonds, walnuts), and seeds (flaxseeds) for a healthy dose of fiber, protein, and healthy fats (F, P, H)
  • Coconut Water (250 ml): Provides electrolytes for hydration (Electrolytes)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a lemon dressing (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Homemade Vegan Energy Bites (2-3): Made with rolled oats, nut butter, dates, and dried fruits for a boost of energy and healthy fats (F, H)
  • (Sattu) drink (200 ml): A roasted gram flour beverage rich in protein, iron, and fiber (P, Iron, F)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for a complete meal (C, F, Vitamins)
  • Palak Paneer (150 gms) with Tofu instead of Paneer: Spinach gravy with tofu for protein, calcium alternative, and iron (P, Ca, Iron)
  • Raita made with curd (coconut yogurt) (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm herbal tea (Chamomile/Ashwagandha) (200 ml): Promotes relaxation and sleep (may help with menstrual cramps)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

  • Focus on iron-rich foods during your period to combat fatigue:
    Sprouted lentils salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and lemon dressing (Iron, F, Vitamins)
    Dates (2-3): A natural source of iron and energy (Iron, C)
    Dark leafy greens like spinach (in salads or stir-fries) (Iron)
  • Ginger tea (200 ml) with honey (optional) can help with cramps (may reduce inflammation)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
