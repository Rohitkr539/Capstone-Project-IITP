23 Year Girl


A sample meal plan for an 23-year-old girl focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (150 gms cooked): Made with sprouted moong dal (lentils) and vegetables (spinach, onions) for protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Coconut Yogurt (150 gms) with chopped fruits (berries, banana) (50 gms): Provides probiotics for gut health, healthy fats, and vitamins (Probiotics, H, Vitamins)
  • Chia Seed Pudding (150 gms): Chia seeds soaked in plant-based milk (like almond milk) with chopped nuts and berries for protein, fiber, healthy fats, and B vitamins (P, F, H, B vitamins)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Foxnut (Makhana) Salad (100 gms): : Roasted foxnuts tossed with chopped vegetables (cucumber, tomato), lemon juice, and spices for a light and crunchy snack (F, Vitamins)
  • Fresh Dates (2-3): A natural source of energy with some fiber and minerals (C, F, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Vegetable Korma (150 gms): Mixed vegetables cooked in a creamy coconut milk gravy (F, Vitamins)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Dal Tadka (150 gms): Lentils cooked with spices, a good source of plantbased protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Vegetable Samosa (1 medium) with chutney: Baked or air-fried for a healthier option. Provides fiber and some protein (F, P)
  • Roasted chickpeas (50 gms): A good source of plant-based protein and fiber (P, F)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Mushroom Matar Masala (150 gms): Mushrooms and peas cooked in a flavorful gravy for protein and iron (P, Fe)
  • Methi Roti (2 medium): Flatbread made with whole wheat flour and fenugreek leaves for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm plant-based milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and supports stress management (Ca - fortified plant-based milk, Antioxidants)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

  • Iron-rich foods (daily): Dark leafy greens (spinach, kale), lentils, tofu, fortified cereals (Fe)
  • Calcium-rich foods (daily): Fortified plant-based milk, ragi flour, sesame seeds (Ca)
  • Ginger tea (2-3 cups daily): Soothes cramps and eases nausea (Antiinflammatory)
  • Warm compress on the abdomen: Provides relief from cramps

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
