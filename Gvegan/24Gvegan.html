24 Year Girl


A sample meal plan for an 24-year-old girl focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (150 gms cooked):: Made with sprouted moong dal and vegetables (spinach, onions) for protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Coconut Yogurt (150 gms) with chopped berries (50 gms) and a sprinkle of chia seeds (20 gms): Provides probiotics, healthy fats, and antioxidants (Probiotics, H, Antioxidants)
  • A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and chia seeds for healthy fats and B vitamins (H, B vitamins)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Fruit Salad (150 gms): A mix of seasonal fruits like mango, guava, and papaya for vitamins and fiber (Vitamins, F)
  • Roasted Makhana (foxnuts) (20 gms): A healthy source of protein and calcium (P, Ca)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Stir-fried Seasonal Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a lemon dressing (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Vegetable Samosa (1 medium) with chutney: Made with whole wheat flour and filled with spiced vegetables for a satisfying snack (F, C)
  • Soy Milk (200 ml) fortified with Calcium and B12: Provides protein, calcium, and essential vitamin B12 (P, Ca, B12)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for a complete meal (C, F, Vitamins)
  • Palak Paneer (150 gms): Spinach and tofu in a creamy gravy, rich in protein and iron (P, Fe)
  • Raita made with plant-based yogurt (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Herbal tea (Chamomile or Lavender): Promotes relaxation and restful sleep

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

  • Include iron-rich foods throughout your cycle:
    Tofu scramble with spinach (100 gms each) for breakfast (P, Fe)
    Rajma Masala with brown rice (lunch) - mentioned above (P, Fe, C)
    Dates (2-3) as a mid-afternoon snack (natural sugars, Fe)
  • Include cramp-reducing foods:
    Ginger tea (throughout the day) - soothes cramps
    Papaya (100 gms) with breakfast or as a snack (provides papain enzyme)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
