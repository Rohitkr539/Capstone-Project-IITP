27 Year Women


A sample meal plan for an 27-year-old women focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (150 gms cooked): Made with gram flour (besan) and sprouted moong dal for protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Fresh Fruits (1 cup chopped): Seasonal fruits like mango, banana, or berries for vitamins and antioxidants (Vitamins, Antioxidants)
  • Plant-Based Milk (200 ml): Fortified with calcium and Vitamin B12 (Ca, B12)
  • Chia Seeds (1 tbsp): Rich in omega-3 fatty acids and fiber (H, F)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Roasted Makhana (foxnuts) (50 gms): A healthy source of protein and calcium (P, Ca)
  • Dates (2-3): Natural source of energy with some fiber and minerals (C, F, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Aloo Gobi Sabzi (150 gms): Stir-fried potatoes and cauliflower with spices for vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a lemon dressing (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Vegetable Bhel (100 gms): Puffed rice tossed with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber, onion), peanuts, and chutneys for protein, fiber, and healthy fats (P, F, H)
  • Trail mix (50 gms): Made with a combination of nuts (almonds, walnuts), seeds (flaxseeds, pumpkin seeds), and dried fruits (raisins, cranberries) for protein, healthy fats, fiber, and iron (P, H, F, Fe)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Palak Paneer (150 gms) made with tofu instead of paneer: Provides protein, calcium, and iron (P, Ca, Fe)
  • Jowar Roti (2 medium): Flatbread made with Jowar flour (sorghum) for complex carbohydrates and gluten-free option (C)
  • Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Like broccoli or cauliflower for essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm herbal tea (1 cup): Chamomile or peppermint for relaxation (optional)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

  • Ginger tea (1 cup):
    Ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can help ease cramps (during period)
  • Warm milk with turmeric (a pinch) and honey (1 tsp): Provides calcium, soothes cramps, and promotes relaxation (Ca, Antioxidants, C)
  • Fruits rich in Vitamin C and magnesium: Oranges, guava, and bananas can help with cramps and bloating (Vitamins, Minerals)
  • Leafy green vegetables: Spinach and kale are rich in iron, which can be lost during menstruation (Fe)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
