31 Year Women


A sample meal plan for an 31-year-old women focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (150 gms cooked): Made with sprouted moong dal and vegetables (spinach, onions) for protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Coconut Yogurt (150 gms) with chopped berries (50 gms): Provides probiotics and gut health benefits, along with Vitamin C and antioxidants (Probiotics, C, Antioxidants)
  • Chia seed pudding (100 gms): Chia seeds soaked in plant-based milk with chopped nuts and berries for protein, fiber, healthy fats, and Omega-3s (P, F, H, Omega-3s)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Fruit and Nut Butter Wrap (1 whole wheat roti): Spread nut butter (20 gms) on a whole wheat roti and fill with chopped fruits (banana, apple) for protein, healthy fats, and fiber (P, H, F)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Stir-fried Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Spinach, broccoli, or other vegetables cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a lemon dressing (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Roasted Makhana (foxnuts) (50 gms) with a handful of mixed berries (50 gms): Roasted foxnuts for protein and fiber, berries for antioxidants and Vitamin C (P, F, C, Antioxidants)
  • Herbal Tea (1 cup): Choose calming options like chamomile or peppermint for relaxation

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Vegetable Korma (150 gms): Mixed vegetables in a creamy cashew-based gravy for protein and healthy fats (P, H)
  • Quinoa (100 gms cooked): A complete protein source with all essential amino acids (P)
  • Steamed Broccoli (100 gms): Rich in Vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants (C, F, Antioxidants)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm plant-based milk (200 ml) with turmeric (a pinch) and a teaspoon of Ashwagandha powder: Promotes restful sleep, has anti-inflammatory properties, and supports stress management (Ca - if fortified, Antioxidants)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

Focus on iron-rich foods during menstruation to combat potential deficiencies (Iron).

  • Sprouted Lentil Salad (100 gms): Sprouted lentils with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) for protein, fiber, and iron (P, F, Iron).
  • Dates (2-3): A natural source of iron and energy (Iron, C).
  • Beetroot Smoothie (200 ml): Beetroot, banana, plant-based milk, and a sprinkle of flaxseeds for iron, fiber, healthy fats, and Vitamin C (Iron, F, H, C).
  • Herbal teas: Ginger or chamomile tea can help with cramps (may help with cramps).

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
