32 Year Women


A sample meal plan for an 32-year-old women focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Vegetable Cheela (150 gms cooked) made with chickpea flour (besan) and spinach: Provides protein and iron for energy (P, Fe)
  • Dalia Porridge (100 gms cooked) with chopped fruits (berries, banana) (50 gms) and a drizzle of plant-based milk (50 ml): Offers complex carbohydrates, fiber, and gut health benefits (C, F, Probiotics)
  • Flaxseeds (1 tbsp): Rich in omega-3 fatty acids and fiber (H, F)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (cucumber, tomato) and a lemon dressing: Provides protein, fiber, and Vitamin C (P, F, Vitamin C)
  • A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, and pumpkin seeds for healthy fats, B vitamins, and minerals (H, B vitamins, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Kidney Bean Curry (150 gms) with mixed vegetables and brown rice (150 gms cooked): Rich in protein, fiber, and complex carbohydrates (P, F, C)
  • Green leafy vegetable stir-fry (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Fruit Yogurt Parfait (150 gms) made with plant-based yogurt and chopped fruits (berries, banana) (50 gms): Provides gut health benefits, fiber, and vitamins (Probiotics, F, Vitamins)
  • Roasted chickpeas (50 gms): A crunchy and protein-rich snack (P)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked): A flavorful rice dish with mixed vegetables for a complete meal (C, F, Vitamins)
  • Tofu Tikka Masala (150 gms): Marinated tofu in a flavorful gravy for protein and calcium (P, Ca)
  • Raita made with vegan yogurt (100 gms) and chopped cucumber: Provides cooling properties and aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Chamomile tea (250 ml): May help promote relaxation and sleep (may not be suitable for everyone)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

Incorporated during periods:

  • Include iron-rich foods: Dark leafy greens (spinach, kale), lentils, fortified cereals (Fe).
  • Ginger tea (1 cup): Soothes cramps and discomfort (Anti-inflammatory)
  • Warm milk with turmeric (a pinch) and a sprinkle of cinnamon: Promotes relaxation and eases cramps (Anti-inflammatory)
  • Fruits rich in Vitamin C: Oranges, grapefruits, strawberries (Vitamin C)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
