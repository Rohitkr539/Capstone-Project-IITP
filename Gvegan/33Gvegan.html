33 Year Women


A sample meal plan for an 33-year-old women focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Smoothie (250 ml):
    Plant-based milk (unsweetened) (150 ml) - (C, Ca)
    Frozen berries (50 gms) - (Vitamins, Antioxidants)
    Spinach (25 gms) - (F, Vitamins)
    Chia seeds (1 tbsp) - (H, B12)
    Flaxseeds (1 tbsp) - (H, Omega-3)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouted moong dal salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (cucumber, tomato, onion) and lemon dressing - (P, F, Vitamins)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Vegan Keema Curry (150 gms) with minced tofu or lentils in a spiced gravy - (P, C)
  • Brown Rice (100 gms cooked) - (C, F)
  • Roasted seasonal vegetables (100 gms) - (Vitamins, Minerals)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Roasted makhana (foxnuts) (50 gms) with a handful of mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts) (20 gms) - (H, P)
  • Dates (2-3) - (C, Iron)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Vegan Palak Paneer (150 gms) with spinach gravy and tofu or tempeh - (P, Iron, Ca)
  • Jowar roti (2 medium) - (C, F)
  • Salad with mixed greens and a teaspoon of olive oil (100 gms) - (Vitamins, Minerals, H)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Chamomile tea (250 ml): May help promote relaxation and sleep (may not be suitable for everyone)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

  • Include these throughout your cycle, especially during cramps:
    Ginger tea (with a pinch of turmeric) - (Anti-inflammatory)
    Papaya (100 gms) - (Anti-inflammatory)
    Pineapple (100 gms) - (Anti-inflammatory)
    Iron-fortified cereals (50 gms) - (Iron)
    Leafy greens like spinach (50 gms) - (Iron)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
