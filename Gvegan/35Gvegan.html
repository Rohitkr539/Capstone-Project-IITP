35 Year Women


A sample meal plan for an 35-year-old women focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (150 gms cooked): Made with sprouted moong dal and vegetables (spinach, onions) for protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Freshly Squeezed Juice (250 ml): Orange, carrot, or beetroot juice for vitamins and antioxidants (Vitamins, Antioxidants)
  • A handful of mixed nuts and seeds (20 gms): Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds for healthy fats, B vitamins, and fiber (H, B vitamins, F)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Fruit Salad (150 gms): Mix of seasonal fruits (mango, banana, berries) for vitamins and fiber (Vitamins, F)
  • Plant-based Yogurt (150 gms) with a sprinkle of granola (20 gms): Provides probiotics for gut health and fiber (Probiotics, F)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Rajma Masala (150 gms): Kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Spinach or kale cooked with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)
  • Salad with mixed greens (spinach, lettuce) and a lemon dressing (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Vegetable Samosas (2 medium) baked instead of fried: Filled with spiced vegetables for a satisfying snack (F, Vitamins)
  • Coconut Water (250 ml): Provides electrolytes for hydration (Electrolytes)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Chana Masala (150 gms): Chickpea curry, a good source of protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Vegetable Roti (2 medium): Made with whole wheat flour for complex carbohydrates (C)
  • Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides essential vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants (Vitamins, Minerals, Antioxidants)
  • Raita made with plant-based yogurt (100 gms) and mint chutney: Aids digestion (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Warm herbal tea (250 ml): Chamomile or peppermint tea for relaxation (optional)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

Incorporated during period:

  • Iron-rich foods: Focus on including spinach (in salads or curries - 100 gms), tofu (stir-fried or scrambled - 100 gms), and fortified cereals (check labels) to combat iron deficiency which can cause fatigue during periods (Fe).
  • Calcium-rich foods: Include sesame seeds (sprinkle on salads or curries - 1 tablespoon), ragi flour rotis (2 medium), or fortified plant-based milk (250 ml) for cramp relief (Ca).
  • Ginger (2-3 gms) tea: Helps with cramps and inflammation.
  • Stay hydrated: Drinking plenty of water helps reduce bloating.

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
