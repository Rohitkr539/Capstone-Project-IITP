39 Year Women


A sample meal plan for an 39-year-old women focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Sprouted Moong Dal Cheela (150 gms cooked): Made with sprouted moong dal and vegetables (spinach, onions) for protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Seasonal Fruit Salad (100 gms): Provides vitamins and antioxidants (Vitamins, Antioxidants)
  • Plant-based Milk (200 ml) fortified with B12 and Calcium: Provides essential nutrients (Ca, B12)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Roasted foxnuts (makhana) (50 gms): A healthy source of protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Dates (2-3): Natural energy source with fiber and minerals (C, F, Minerals)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Chickpea Curry (150 gms): Rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Brown Rice (150 gms cooked): Provides complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (C)
  • Stir-fried Greens (100 gms): Like spinach or kale for vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)
  • Coconut Chutney (50 gms): Provides healthy fats and a touch of sweetness (H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Vegetable Idli (2 medium) with Sambar (100 gms): Steamed lentil cakes with a lentil stew for protein and fiber (P, F)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Vegetable Khichdi (150 gms cooked): A comforting dish of rice and lentils, good source of protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Tofu Bhurji (150 gms): Scrambled tofu with vegetables for protein and calcium (P, Ca)
  • Roasted Seasonal Vegetables (100 gms): Provides additional vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Chamomile tea (200 ml): May help promote relaxation and sleep (may not be suitable for everyone)

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

  • Focus on iron-rich foods during menstruation:
    Dark Leafy Greens (spinach, kale) (100 gms) in salads or cooked (Fe)
    Rajma Masala (150 gms) with kidney beans (Fe)
  • Include flaxseeds (1 tbsp) daily for Omega-3 fatty acids, which may help with cramps (H)
  • Consume fruits rich in Vitamin C (like oranges, 1 medium) to aid iron absorption (Vitamins)

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
