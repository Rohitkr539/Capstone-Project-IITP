45 Year Elder Women


A sample meal plan for an 45-year-Elder women focused on Indian cuisine with vegan options, rich in nutrients, and promoting overall health during menstruation:

Remember: This is a sample, adjust portion sizes based on activity level and consult a doctor or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Focus on Variety: Include a range of colors in your vegetables and fruits for a diverse nutrient profile.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day (around 3 liters)

Key: (F) - Fiber, (P) - Protein, (C) - Carbohydrates, (H) - Healthy Fats, (Ca) - Calcium, (B12) - Vitamin B12, (Iron) - Iron

Morning (7 AM):

  • Oatmeal (50 gms cooked) with:
    Plant-based milk (200 ml) fortified with calcium and Vitamin D (C, Ca, Vitamin D)
    Berries (50 gms) for antioxidants (Antioxidants)
    Chia seeds (1 tbsp) for omega-3 fatty acids and fiber (H, F)

Mid-Morning Snack (10 AM):

  • Sprouted moong dal salad (100 gms) with chopped vegetables (tomato, cucumber) and lemon dressing (P, F, Vitamins)
         OR
  • Fruit with a handful of nuts (almonds, walnuts) (50 gms each) for protein, healthy fats, and fiber (P, H, F)

Lunch (1 PM):

  • Rajma Masala (150 gms) with kidney beans in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Brown rice (100 gms cooked) for complex carbohydrates (C)
  • Stir-fried greens (spinach, kale) (100 gms) with a teaspoon of olive oil for added vitamins and minerals (Vitamins, Minerals, H)
  • Salad with mixed greens and a teaspoon of flaxseed oil (100 gms) for omega-3 fatty acids (H)

Evening Snack (4 PM):

  • Homemade trail mix (50 gms) with a mix of dried fruits (cranberries, raisins), pumpkin seeds, and sunflower seeds for fiber, healthy fats, and minerals (F, H, Minerals)
  • Soya milk yogurt (150 gms) fortified with calcium and Vitamin D (Ca, Vitamin D)

Dinner (7 PM):

  • Vegetable Biryani (150 gms cooked) with mixed vegetables for complex carbohydrates and fiber (C, F)
  • Chana Masala (150 gms) with chickpeas in a flavorful gravy, rich in protein and fiber (P, F)
  • Raita made with plant-based yogurt (100 gms) and chopped cucumber for cooling properties and gut health (Probiotics)

Before Bed (optional):

  • Chamomile tea (1 cup) for relaxation and sleep support

Foods for Menstrual Cycle Relief (Separate from Regular Diet):

  • Flaxseeds (1 tbsp) daily: Rich in lignans, which may help with hot flashes.
  • Soy products (tofu, tempeh) (1-2 servings a week): Contain plant-based estrogens that may ease some symptoms.
  • Cruciferous vegetables (cauliflower, broccoli) (2-3 servings a week): May help regulate hormones.
  • Fruits rich in antioxidants (berries, oranges) (1-2 servings a day): May reduce inflammation and support overall health.

Alternative Vitamin B12 sources:

  • Fortified cereals: Look for cereals with added B12.
  • Nutritional yeast: A deactivated yeast source rich in B vitamins, sprinkle on salads or add to curries.
  • Fortified plant-based milk: Check labels for B12 fortification.

Disclaimer: This plan provides an example and may not meet all your individual needs. It's recommended to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for a personalized plan considering factors like allergies, activity level, and specific health goals.
